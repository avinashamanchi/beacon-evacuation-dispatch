# BEACON — video kit

Two layers, use either or both:

1. **The product film (already built): `http://localhost:8010/film`** — a 2-minute
   self-playing cinematic that drives the *live* app. Screen-record it once and
   you have the backup video, the booth loop, and the submission clip.
2. **Optional AI-generated b-roll** — the prompts below, written for Sora /
   Veo / Runway, to splice as a 10–15s cold-open before the product film.
   Never generate footage depicting real victims or real events — these are
   all abstract or fictional-town shots.

## How to record the product film

1. `Reset` is automatic — the film resets the incident itself at 0:43.
2. Open `http://localhost:8010/film` in a clean full-screen window (hide bookmarks bar).
3. macOS: `Cmd+Shift+5` → "Record Selected Portion" → drag over the letterboxed
   frame → Record. The film gives you a 3-2-1 countdown before the first scene.
4. It runs ~2:05 and ends on the close card. Stop recording, trim the ends.
5. `Space` pauses mid-take; `R` restarts clean for another take.
6. Voiceover: press `N` for the built-in teleprompter — the full timed
   script lives in [NARRATION.md](NARRATION.md).

## B-roll prompts (in the marketing-prompt style — no real events, no real people)

**Shot 1 — the ember cold-open (3–4s)**
Macro shot of embers rising against pure black, drifting upward in slow
motion with soft turbulence. Extremely shallow depth of field; each ember a
warm orange bokeh point. No flames, no landscape — abstract heat. Slow 10%
push-in. Mood: somber, held-breath, documentary gravity. Cut on a hard
black frame.

**Shot 2 — the silent phone (3s)**
Close-up of a phone on a kitchen table in a dim room, screen glowing with a
typed, unsent message; no faces shown. A faint orange flicker reflects on
the wall behind it — source never shown. Static camera, one subtle focus
pull from the wall to the screen. Mood: quiet dread, restraint. No text
legible — the words are ours to supply in the caption.

**Shot 3 — the dispatch console (4s)**
Over-the-shoulder shot of a darkened operations room, silhouetted operator
before a wall of warm amber monitors, rack lights blinking. Slow lateral
dolly right, monitors out of focus — readable UI comes from our real
product footage, not this shot. Mood: professional, high-stakes calm.
Cool shadows against warm screen light.

**Shot 4 — the empty road (3s)**
Aerial slow push over an empty two-lane road cutting through dark pine
ridgeline at dusk, hazy air, no fire visible — only an orange glow on the
horizon line. Fictional terrain, no landmarks. Mood: scale, time running
out, isolation.

**Assembly:** Shot 1 → Shot 2 → hard cut to the product film's evidence
cards (its black background makes the cut seamless) → the film carries the
rest. B-roll totals ≤15s; the product does the talking.

**Guardrails:** no real victims' names, no reenactment of any real 911 call,
no real-town landmarks. On the evidence cards the finding is attributed to
the Eaton fire; everything visual is abstract or fictional. Say it out loud
on stage too: "the finding is real; the town on screen is fictional."
